Course Description / Rationale ▼

What this course covers

TAS1O is a hands-on course where students explore technology and the skilled trades through real projects. In particular, learners practise the engineering design process and build practical skills with tools, materials, and industry-style techniques.

At the same time, students focus on precision measurement and safe work habits. As projects grow in complexity, they also learn how to test ideas, improve prototypes, and communicate results clearly.

Finally, the course introduces job skills programs, training pathways, and trade-related careers. Therefore, students can make informed choices about future education and workplace options.

Course Content (Strands & Hours) ▼

Time plan

  • Strand A: Design Processes and Related Skills — 50 hours
  • Strand B: Technological Development, Impacts, and Careers — 50 hours
  • Summative Task — 10 hours
  • Total: 110 hours
Strand A — Overall Expectations ▼

Design processes and related skills

  • A1. Initiating and Planning: demonstrate an understanding of fundamental technological concepts by initiating and planning projects.
  • A2. Designing and Performing: develop projects that create products and/or services using a variety of resources and techniques, while recording development.
  • A3. Analyzing and Refining: evaluate and refine processes, products, and/or services.
  • A4. Health and Safety: apply health and safety practices and procedures when using materials, tools, and equipment.
Strand B — Overall Expectations ▼

Technology development, impacts, and careers

  • B1. Fundamentals of Technological Development: explain how needs and social, economic, and environmental factors drive technological change.
  • B2. Impacts of Technology: analyze impacts of technologies on individuals, society, the economy, and the environment.
  • B3. Careers and Pathways: explore careers in technology and skilled trades, and describe education and training pathways.
Teaching & Learning Strategies ▼

How learning happens

Learning is active and project-based. For example, students plan designs, build prototypes, test outcomes, and then refine their work. In addition, discussion and reflection support stronger decision-making.

  • Direct instruction and teacher-facilitated class activities
  • Experiential learning (learn by doing)
  • Class, small-group, and partner discussions
  • Worksheets/surveys, brainstorming, and inquiry tasks
  • Independent work and group work with guidance
  • Use of computers/Internet where appropriate
Assessment & Evaluation Breakdown ▼

How grades are calculated

Assessment supports improvement throughout the course. Therefore, students receive feedback and coaching, monitor progress, and reflect on next steps. Meanwhile, evaluation summarizes achievement using consistent criteria.

  • Categories weighting: Knowledge & Understanding (20%), Thinking (30%), Communication (20%), Application (30%)
  • Term work: 70% (evaluations across the course)
  • Final evaluation: 30% (end-of-course summative evaluation)
  • Evidence of learning: products, observations, and conversations
Program Planning Considerations (Summary) ▼

Important supports

Program planning builds accessibility, inclusion, and student success into every stage of learning. For instance, instruction may include visual supports, modeling, hands-on practice, and step-by-step routines.

In addition, students practise collaboration and problem solving through iterative design. As a result, they learn that technology often has multiple correct solutions and requires perseverance.

Safety is also essential. Therefore, students follow clear procedures when using tools, equipment, and materials in learning activities.
