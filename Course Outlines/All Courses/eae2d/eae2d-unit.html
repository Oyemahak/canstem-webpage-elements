1. Short Stories ▼
  • Read a wide range of short stories and analyze themes, plot, and character.
  • Write responses and analytical paragraphs using evidence from the text.
  • Create an original short story using structure, voice, and imagination.
  • Time: 24 Hours
2. Poetry ▼
  • Study poetic devices such as stanzas, rhyme scheme, imagery, and sound.
  • Explore different forms of poetry and how poets engage the reader’s senses.
  • Write original poems, practise reading aloud, and build presentation skills.
  • Complete a final poetry anthology.
  • Time: 20 Hours
3. Shakespeare Drama ▼
  • Learn about Shakespeare’s life and the purpose of drama as tragedy or comedy.
  • Analyze themes, structure, and key features of the play through guided study.
  • Use drama terminology such as allusion, dramatic irony, soliloquy, aside, and iambic pentameter.
  • Strengthen oral communication through listening tasks, dramatic readings, and short presentations.
  • Time: 25 Hours
4. Novel Study ▼
  • Analyze an assigned novel using literary terms (theme, setting, conflict, plot, purpose).
  • Use discussion, homework, and presentations to deepen understanding of the text.
  • Explore historical and socio-cultural context through research tasks.
  • Practise correct citation, acknowledge sources, and avoid plagiarism.
  • Time: 25 Hours
5. Media and Culminating Unit ▼
  • Study how media form and content work together to communicate meaning.
  • Create a media product (tabloid, newscast, magazine, or storyboard/script plan).
  • Use planning, drafting, and revision to improve clarity and impact.
  • Time: 15 Hours
  • Total Course Hours: 110 Hours
Evaluation Breakdown ▼
  • 70% - Term Work (assignments, quizzes, tests, presentations, projects)
  • 30% - Final Evaluation (summative + exam)
  • Knowledge & Understanding - 25%
  • Thinking - 25%
  • Communication - 25%
  • Application - 25%
Teaching & Learning Strategies ▼
  • Teacher-led instruction, class discussion, and guided reading activities
  • Writing workshops, peer feedback, and conferencing
  • Group work, presentations, and oral communication practice
  • Research tasks with a focus on credible sources and citation skills
Resources ▼
  • Course readings (short stories, poems, drama, and an assigned novel)
  • Teacher-provided notes, handouts, and writing supports
  • Online research tools and library resources for context and inquiry
