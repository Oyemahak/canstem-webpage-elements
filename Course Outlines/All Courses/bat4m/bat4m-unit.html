Course Description ▼

What BAT4M focuses on

BAT4M Financial Accounting Principles develops practical skills for financial statements and business reporting. In addition, students learn how accountants record and organize transactions.

How students apply learning

Next, students compare inventory methods and practise adjusting and reversing entries. Finally, students explore business financing and use annual reports to support decisions.

1. The Accounting Cycle ▼

Core accounting skills

  • Learn key accounting principles and daily practices.
  • Complete the accounting cycle for service and merchandising companies.
  • Use internal controls to reduce errors and protect records.
  • Read financial statements and explain what they show.
  • Time: 30 Hours
2. Advanced Accounting Practices for Assets ▼

Assets and inventory decisions

  • Record short-term assets with clear procedures.
  • Track capital assets and apply correct methods.
  • Compare inventory valuation methods and explain the results.
  • Time: 25 Hours
3. Financing ▼

Funding choices

  • Explain how partnerships raise and manage funds.
  • Explain how corporations raise capital and report it.
  • Compare financing options and choose the best fit.
  • Time: 25 Hours
4. Financial Analysis and Decision Making ▼

Reading annual reports

  • Use annual reports to identify strengths and risks.
  • Apply tools to analyze and compare financial data.
  • Discuss current issues in accounting and reporting.
  • Time: 25 Hours
Evaluation Breakdown ▼

How marks are calculated

  • 70% — Course work (tests, assignments, and tasks)
  • 30% — Final Exam (culminating activity)
  • Achievement Chart: Knowledge & Understanding (25%), Thinking (25%), Communication (25%), Application (25%)
Learning Skills ▼

Skills students build throughout the course

  • Responsibility
  • Organization
  • Independent Work
  • Collaboration
  • Initiative
  • Self-Regulation
Assessment Approach ▼

How teachers support learning

  • Assessment for Learning: teachers give feedback and coaching.
  • Assessment as Learning: students set goals and reflect regularly.
  • Assessment of Learning: graded tasks build the final mark.
  • Evidence of Learning: products, observations, and conversations.
Late Assignments / Missed Evaluations ▼

Submission expectations

  • The teacher may deduct 5% per school day for late work.
  • If a student misses a test, the teacher may assign zero without documentation.
  • If absences continue, the teacher may refer the case to administration.
