About ILO
The International Logic Olympiad started in 2024 as a partnership between Stanford University and Luminas, a 501(c)(3) non-profit organization.

Just like math and language, logic exists in the foundational fabric of society and is utilized perhaps even more so. However, logic is still severely underrepresented in modern education. Due to this gap, we started the ILO to highlight the importance of logical reasoning and problem-solving.
About Luminas
Luminas is a 501(c)(3) non-profit organization dedicated to providing experiential learning opportunities to students worldwide.

Our mission is create transformative experiences where students can showcase their unique skills and brilliant minds on the global stage.

Through promoting leadership, problem-solving, and collaboration, Luminas ensures that students from all backgrounds have equal access to educational opportunities based on their merits.

What is ILO, and what is CanSTEM’s role?

A simple overview for families

The International Logic Olympiad (ILO) is a global logic competition that builds reasoning and problem-solving. Because of that, it is a great fit for students who enjoy thinking challenges.

CanSTEM Education is an official ILO Regional Partner & Testing Center (Canada). Therefore, we help students train and stay on track with clear weekly goals.

What ILO tests

ILO focuses on logic, careful reading, patterns, and sets. However, it rewards clear steps more than memorization.

  • Logic puzzles & constraints
  • Pattern recognition
  • Set reasoning (union/intersection/complement)
  • Step-by-step solutions

CanSTEM support

We help families plan from start to finish. In addition, we train students with mock exams and pacing.

  • Coaching + mock tests
  • Division placement guidance
  • Test-day readiness support
  • Online coaching across Canada

Why students enjoy it

It feels like smart puzzles with a goal. Therefore, many students stay motivated and improve quickly.

  • Fun problem sets
  • Confidence with new question types
  • Stronger thinking skills
  • Better calm under time

Frequently asked questions (FAQs)

Common questions from families

Are you an official ILO Regional Partner?
Yes. CanSTEM Education supports ILO as an official Regional Partner & Testing Center (Canada). Also, we help students prepare with coaching and guide families through key steps.
Where can we try official ILO-style questions?
Use the official sample test here: portal.logicolympiad.org/sample. Then, if you want faster progress, join coaching for guided practice.
What are the key dates for 2026?
Registration opens Jan 14, 2026 and closes Mar 18, 2026. After that, preliminary rounds run in April (Junior Apr 18, Senior Apr 25). Finally, finals are July 1–3, 2026 at Stanford University, USA (for qualifiers).
What is the registration fee?
The official ILO registration fee is $50 (paid through the official portal). However, coaching fees are separate.
Do you offer online coaching across Canada?
Yes. We provide live online sessions across Canada. In addition, we offer in-person coaching in Brampton.
How do we start?
Submit the inquiry form with your child’s grade and goals. Then we recommend a plan and schedule. After that, we begin weekly training and mock exams closer to the preliminary round.
