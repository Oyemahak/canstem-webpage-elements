FAQs

Is summer school only for students who failed a course?
No. Many students join summer school to improve marks, complete a credit early, reduce next year’s workload, or strengthen their academic profile before university or college applications.
What are the main benefits of taking summer school in Grade 9 to 12?
Summer school can help students stay on track for graduation, improve marks in key subjects, complete prerequisites earlier, and create a lighter timetable during the regular school year.
Do students usually take summer school for credit recovery or mark upgrades?
Both. Some students need to recover a missing credit, while others use summer school to upgrade marks in courses that matter for future academic pathways.
How do families decide between online and in-class summer school?
Families usually compare flexibility, commute, learning style, and the level of structure a student needs. Online learning works well for convenience, while in-class study can be better for students who want face-to-face support.
Why do students look for summer school before the next academic year starts?
Because it gives them a chance to solve academic gaps early, improve confidence, and begin the next school year with fewer course pressures and better preparation.
